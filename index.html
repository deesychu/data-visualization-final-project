Get Out! A Data-Driven Narrative of
Housing Displacement in San Francisco

By Adrienne Dominique Sy Chu

San Francisco has long struggled with a housing crisis that has forced thousands of residents out of their homes. Eviction notices tell the story of who is being displaced, where it is happening, and why — but raw data alone can be hard to understand. This project turns nearly three decades of eviction records from the SF Rent Board into a visual story that anyone can follow, making the numbers feel real and helping everyday people understand one of San Francisco's most pressing issues.

Data: SF Rent Arbitration Board via DataSF · Eviction Notices (1997–2025)

Eviction Notices by SF Neighborhood (1997–2025)

Choropleth map of geographic concentration of eviction notices across San Francisco's 41 neighborhoods

Housing displacement in San Francisco is deeply uneven, with historically high-density rental areas bearing a disproportionate share of eviction filings over the past three decades. The Mission District alone accounts for over 11% of all citywide notices filed since 1997. Use the year slider to trace how the geography of displacement has shifted in response to economic cycles, gentrification, and policy changes.

Eviction Causes by SF Neighborhood (1997–2025)

Heatmap of eviction cause concentrations across San Francisco's 20 highest-volume neighborhoods, sorted by total filings

The causes driving eviction are not distributed evenly — each neighborhood carries a distinct displacement fingerprint shaped by its housing stock and demographics. Owner Move-In and Breach filings dominate high-density corridors like the Mission, while Nuisance evictions concentrate in areas with larger multi-unit buildings. These 20 neighborhoods account for over 80% of all citywide notices filed since 1997.

★ Darker cells (top ~40% by volume per cause) are interactive — hover to reveal neighborhood context.

SF Eviction Notices Filed Per Year (1997–2025)

Line chart of annual eviction filings reflecting the economic pressures shaping San Francisco's housing crisis

Over nearly three decades, San Francisco's eviction filings have tracked closely with economic booms and crises — surging in the dot-com era, collapsing after 2001, and spiking again during the mid-2010s tech boom. The COVID-19 moratorium caused the sharpest single-year drop on record in 2020. Hover any data point or annotation to explore the historical context behind each shift.

Eviction Notices by Cause Across Each Month (All Years Combined)

Stacked bar chart of eviction types by month, revealing seasonal patterns in San Francisco displacement

Eviction filings in San Francisco follow distinct seasonal rhythms tied to the rental market cycle. Owner Move-In notices peak in spring as landlords align displacement with summer lease turnover, while Breach and Nuisance filings climb in late summer and fall. Hover any bar to read a focused seasonal insight for that month.
