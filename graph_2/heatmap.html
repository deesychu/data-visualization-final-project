Eviction Causes by SF Neighborhood (1997–2025)

Heatmap of eviction cause concentrations across San Francisco's 20 highest-volume neighborhoods, sorted by total filings

The causes driving eviction are not distributed evenly across San Francisco's neighborhoods, with each area carries a distinct displacement fingerprint shaped by its housing stock, tenant demographics, and proximity to economic pressure. Owner Move-In and Breach filings dominate high-density residential corridors like the Mission, Sunset/Parkside, and Outer Richmond, while Nuisance-based evictions are disproportionately concentrated in areas with larger multi-unit buildings and transient populations. The 20 neighborhoods shown here account for over 80% of all eviction notices filed citywide since 1997; hover the darkest cells to explore the stories behind each neighborhood's dominant eviction cause.

Source: SF Rent Arbitration Board via DataSF · Eviction Notices dataset (as of April 2026)
