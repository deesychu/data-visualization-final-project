SF Eviction Notices Filed Per Year (1997–2025)

Annual eviction filings reflect the economic pressures shaping San Francisco's housing crisis over nearly three decades

Over the past 28 years, San Francisco's eviction landscape has been shaped by a succession of economic booms, financial crises, and public health emergencies, each leaving a distinct imprint on the pace of displacement. Filings surged during the late 1990s dot-com era, collapsed after the 2001 bust, and climbed again through the mid-2010s tech boom before plummeting during COVID-19 in 2020. The post-pandemic period reveals a city still in recovery, with filings remaining well below pre-pandemic highs even as housing pressure has intensified, which is a pattern that raises urgent questions about the sustainability of current tenant protections.

Source: SF Rent Arbitration Board via DataSF · Eviction Notices dataset (as of April 2026)
