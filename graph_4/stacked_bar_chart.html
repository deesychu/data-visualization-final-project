Eviction Notices by Cause Across Each Month (All Years Combined)

Certain eviction types spike during specific months, revealing seasonal patterns in how and when San Francisco residents are displaced

Eviction filings in San Francisco follow distinct seasonal rhythms that reflect the underlying motivations of landlords and the pressures of the city's rental market cycle. Owner Move-In notices surge sharply in the spring, peaking in April through June, as landlords time displacement to coincide with the end of academic years and the start of summer lease turnover. Breach and Nuisance filings climb in late summer and fall, while Non-Payment notices rise in September and again in December, tracking closely with economic stress points in the rental calendar.

Source: SF Rent Arbitration Board via DataSF · Eviction Notices dataset (as of April 2026)
